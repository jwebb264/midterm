5 Most Popular Dogs in the US
Breed Personality Size Age
Labrador Retriever Labs are basically the short-haired, crazy cousin of goldens. They make excellent family dogs, love to swim, and are extremely trainable. They're known for eating things they shouldn't so keep an eye on your socks. Large breed: 50-80 lbs 11-13 years
French Bulldog This breed is good for people who live in a smaller area, as they shed less and take up less space than a large breed. They're excellent with children, but due to their small size, should be supervised with young kids. They are a brachycephalic breed and tend to have health issues related to their snout shape. Small breed: up to 28 lbs 10-12 years
Golden Retriever Goldens are well-known family dogs and are likely to love everyone. They are terrible guard dogs, but are excellent with children of all ages. They are known as "velcro dogs" and want to be with their owners all the time. Large breed: 55-75 lbs 10-12 years
German Shepherd An excellent family watchdog, shepards bond closely with their family. They are excellent with children of all ages but have a tendency to bark. Unlike other breeds, Shepards tend to be aloof initially and do not warm up to strangers for a bit. Large breed: 50-90 lbs. 7-10 years
Poodle Excellent family dog. However, this dog is high energy and extremely intelligent. Poodles of all sizes require a job to be happy. This dog does not shed and will need to be groomed regularly to prevent matting. Bred in toy, miniature, and standard sizes. Standard: 40-70 lbs 10-18 years
